How to plan

So people are born with structured mind, some not, but it doesn't mean you can't learn how to make things logic and at their place.

Trip planning is just like cleaning the house: you need to know where the exact place for each thing.

Firstly, it seems very complicated, but practice makes perfect as we all know.

So how to make trip planning easier for newbie?

When, where, what?

Let's start with this 3 simple question:

  • When do you plan to travel?
  • Where do you want to go?
  • What do you want from this trip?

Why this is so important? Because you want a trip of your dream and it's better to visit country in the most suitable time and season for you.

There is no need to repeat that you need to keep in mind school vacations, weather forecast and national holidays to avoid tons of people and queues at every sightseeing.

How much money I can spend on holiday?

Please be honest to yourself. If you can't live without morning coffee, you can't change it during your vacation. If you don't have much spendings, don't book a 5-star hotel, you can't afford it. As well as expensive restaurants and shops.

Here is my tips of how to spend less on vacations. Not everything should be suitable for you, but at least I tried:

  1. Hostels are good! Don't be afraid of using it, just read reviews and ratings
  2. Try couchsurfing if you are brave and want to meet new people. But take a closer look ar reviews
  3. Choose hotel or appartment near your aproximate route. It may looks a bit expensive, but you can save on public transport and time
  4. Book apartments and hotel rooms with teapot and microwave - it can save you a lot of money on meals as well as the nearest food market
  5. If you coffee/tea/etc addicted, take it from home. Just don't break the custom rules of the place of visit
  6. Don't use taxi in airport. Most citizens use public transport, be smart, be like them
  7. Get a rental car if you plan to travel between several cities - it's always cheaper especially if you are travelling in group

What I normally include in the final plan to avoid anxiety?

  1. List of 2-3 flights suitable for your budget and timing
  2. Several hotels/hostels with good rating near the selected area
  3. Useful tips like "what you need to avoid doing in this country and why"
  4. Some places to eat/drink near you hotel/hostel
  5. Daily plan with:
    • How to get to city center from airport/railway station
    • List of sights in this area
    • Pricing for all activities you plan to attend
    • Google map/MAPS.ME route with all places mentioned
