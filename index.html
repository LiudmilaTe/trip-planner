How to plan

I'm 100% sure everyone can do planning, you need just to start. And as someone starts their day with coffee and cupcake, let's start practicing planning with the sweetest - vacation. If you're afraid of planning full holiday trip, just start with weekend in the nearest city.

I won't annoying you with tons of texts, but there are my best practices I learnt during all these years planning vacations.

Be honest with yourself

I suggest to start planing with this 5 simple questions:

  • When do I plan to travel?
  • What do I want to see/visit?
  • Why do I want this vacation
  • What is my expectations?
  • How much money I can spend?

So, as you can see, first 4 questions are about your feelings and motivation. Why is it so important? Because we all have this ideal pictures in our mind and we want them to become true, but reality is cruel, so you need to focus on things that are important only for you.

So, if you plan to visit country or city your friend or colleague told you, please think twice: do youhave the same view of life and vacation as your friend/colleague has?

Do you really want to spend a week on a beach, even if your dreams are all about mountains? So, once again, please be honest to yourself.

Now let's talk about money.
Keep in mind, it's almost impossible to spend less money than you spent in everyday life. And if you can't live without morning coffee, you can't change it during your vacation.

Also think about how you plan to spend your savings? Luxury hotel? Fancy restaurant? Or maybe you want to visit as much sightseeing as possible? Find your true desires and try to focus on them. Sometimes to go out of your comfort zone can be enjoying and brings a lot of warming memeries.

As we all care about money, these are my tips of how to spend less on vacations. Not everything should be suitable for you, but at least I tried:

  1. Hostels are good! Don't be afraid of using it, just read reviews and ratings
  2. Try couchsurfing if you are brave or want to meet new people. But take a closer look ar reviews
  3. Choose hotel or appartment near your aproximate route. It may looks a bit expensive, but you can save on public transport and time
  4. Book apartments and hotel rooms with teapot and microwave - it can save you a lot of money on meals as well as the nearest food market
  5. If you coffee/tea/etc addicted, take it from home. Just don't break the custom rules of the place of visit
  6. Don't use taxi in airport. Most citizens use public transport, be smart, be like them
  7. Get a rental car if you plan to travel between several cities - it's always cheaper especially if you are travelling in group

Well, now you have some theory, let's deep dive into practice

Create your first plan

Let's imagine, you want to visit United Kingdom and spend 1 week in September here. Now we have place and timeframe.

The next step is to write your preferences: museums, city walks, reataurants, nature sightseeing. Did it? Great!

Now let's google the most interesting places depends on your categories. It can be everything: most popular places and museums, or super unique pub in the middle of Wales.

Got a list? Now let's place all this sightseeing on a map and arrange them that way, so we'll have several lists of places located near each other.

Well, we almost done. Now you have a daily schedule with places you can visit walking from one to another. If you think it's too much for 1 day - just move some places to another days also according to location.

Booking

This is the most boring part, in my opinion, but it's absolutely neccesary.

Flights

  • If you can move your vacation on 1-2 days, use float search. For example, you can find the cheapest flights by searching the whole month schedule
  • If you want to lower the ticket costs, try to search budget companies that flight from your country
  • Take only carry-on luggage, it's cheaper and you won't spend time waiting for your luggage. But be careful with bag sizes, it's different from company to company
  • Friday, Sunday and Monday tickets are usually the most expensive
  • The best prices are 6-12 weeks before flight date

Accomodation

  • Look at your map with all sightseeing and find a place in center. This is the perfect location for your hotel/hostel

  • Use airbnb or booking to search rooms and ratings. Don't skip reviews, sometimes you can find really useful tips there
  • Pay atention to taxes and other additional expences. It can increase the cost dramaticaly

Way from/to airport

  • Taxi is the first and most obvious choice, but also the most expensive
  • Locals know the cheapest way to get from airport to city center. Just google it
  • If you trip as a big company - book a van in advance
  • If oyu plan to move between cities and towns, it's better to book a rental car. But first google the prices of local buses

To sumarize all written above, what I normally have during my trip planning:

  1. List of 2-3 flights suitable for my budget and timing
  2. Several hotels/hostels with good rating near the selected area
  3. Useful tips like "what you need to avoid doing in this country and why"
  4. Some places to eat/drink near you hotel/hostel
  5. Daily plan with:
    • How to get to city center from airport/railway station
    • Daily list of sightseeings in this area
    • Pricing for all activities you plan to attend
    • Google map/MAPS.ME route with all places mentioned

As you can see, it's not so difficult, when you know what and how to do plannings.
