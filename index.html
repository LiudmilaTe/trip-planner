Tips and tricks about travel planning

Read my guide and save some helpful notes of trip planning!

Hi, I'm Liudmila

and I love planning. All my trips were prepared by me carefully and with love to details.

I started travelling 10 years ago with my first trip to Barcelona. By now I visited 26 countries and more than 60 cities.

How to plan

I don't want to teach you how to travel, you can find plenty of free or paid guides in Google. I just want to share my best practices in case it can be helpful for you.

Be honest with yourself

Before each vacation, I usually ask myself this 3 simple questions:

  • What do I want to see/visit?
  • Why do I want this vacation?
  • What is my expectations?

Why is it so important?

Because we all have this ideal pictures in our mind and we want them to become true, but reality is cruel, so you need to focus on things that are important for you.

If you plan to visit country or city your friend or colleague told you, please think twice: do you have the same view of life and vacation as your friend/colleague has?

Let's talk about money.

Keep in mind, it's almost impossible to spend less money than you spent in everyday life. And if you can't live without morning coffee, you can't change it during your vacation.

Think, what is importnat for you? Luxury hotel? Fancy restaurant? Or maybe you want to visit as much sightseeings as possible? Find your true desires and try to focus on them.

My tips how to spend less on vacations. Not everything should be suitable for you, but at least I tried:

  • Hostels are good! Don't be afraid of using it, just read reviews and ratings
  • Try couchsurfing if you are brave or want to meet new people. But take a closer look ar reviews
  • Choose hotel or appartment near your aproximate route. It may looks a bit expensive, but you can save on public transport and time
  • Book apartments and hotel rooms with teapot and microwave - it can save you a lot of money on meals as well as the nearest food market
  • If you coffee/tea/etc addicted, take it from home. Just don't break the custom rules of the place of visit
  • Don't use taxi in airport. Most citizens use public transport, be smart, be like them
  • Get a rental car if you plan to travel between several cities - it's always cheaper especially if you are travelling in group

Well, now you have some theory, let's deep dive into practice.

Create your first plan

Let's imagine, you want to visit country "C" and spend 1 week here. Now we have place and timeframe.

  • Write your preferences: museums, city walks, reataurants, nature sightseeing. Did it? Great!
  • Google the most interesting places depends on your categories. It can be everything: most popular places, museums, or unique pub and concert
  • Got a list? Place all this sightseeing on a map and arrange them, so you'll have several lists of places located near each other
  • If you think it's too much for 1 day - just move some places to another days also according to location
  • Don't forget to add entry price for each place if available

Well, we're almost done. Now you have a daily schedule with places you can visit walking from one to another.

Booking

In my opinion this is the most boring part, but it's absolutely neccesary.

Flights

  • If you can move your vacation on 1-2 days, use float search. For example, you can find the cheapest flights by searching the whole month schedule
  • If you want to lower the ticket costs, try to search budget companies that flight from your country
  • Take only carry-on luggage, it's cheaper and you won't spend time waiting for your luggage. But be careful with bag sizes, it's different from company to company
  • Friday, Sunday and Monday tickets are usually the most expensive
  • The best prices are 6-12 weeks before flight date

Accomodation

  • Look at your map with all sightseeing and find a place in center. This is the perfect location for your hotel/hostel
  • Use airbnb or booking to search rooms and ratings. Don't skip reviews, sometimes you can find really useful tips there
  • Pay atention to taxes and other additional expences. It can increase the cost dramaticaly

Way from/to airport

  • Taxi is the first and most obvious choice, but also the most expensive
  • Locals know the cheapest way to get from airport to city center. Just google it
  • If you trip as a big company - book a van in advance
  • If you move between cities and towns, it's better to book a rental car. But first google the prices of local buses
